That's a lot, it's overwhelming, it's a lifetime commitment but I digress.

Apparently, in the programming world, there exist kata. Kata in martial arts means form. It's something you go through at the start of every practice session to commit those forms to muscle memory. Peter Provost claims that the only way to learn TDD is through kata. The advise is to pick one kata, do it for 30 minutes every day for two weeks until you get comfortable with how TDD flows.

Don't even start doing TDD on real projects before you're comfortable with TDD. I guess my ultimatum won't be ultimate after all, but I'm glad it lead me to this.

I will start doing it. Starting today (1st of May 2014).
