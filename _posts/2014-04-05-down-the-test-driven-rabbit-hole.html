In January I ran across a blog titled "Programmers without TDD will be unemployable by 2022". Being a programmer, and not knowing what TDD is, I had every reason to panic.

A month later Rocky, my coworker, saw in an internal company survey that I was interested in TDD as a topic. I alone. Rocky's a really nice and talented guy so he offered to teach me TDD "But it would be great if you can find others so we can make it a group session". So I did an office-wide blast of email and got my group, Rocky did the session, complete with homework... which nobody submitted and we all forgot about it and went our merry way. Until...

 

Last night, I came across this quora. I saw android-fu which looks outdated, AndroidAnnotations which (correct me if I'm wrong) requires your whole team to learn it so that you can use it at a functional level, and among the libraries, RoboJuice (correctly spelled RoboGuice, but I need to tell my brain to start saying RoboJuice so that's how I'll write it).

The pitch for RoboJuice and AndroidAnnotations were the same: "Cleaner code, starting in onCreate"* but RoboJuice got me good. Maybe it was the number of Top Developer apps on their wiki page that use it.

I started reading the guides, and always ALWAYS injection was the topic. "What is this dependency injection?", I asked myself. I'm not gonna answer that here, but for you here's a good place to start. As for myself, I took a detour through Quora and got this list. It's even got a link to the blog post by the god who coined the term.

 

Apparently dependency injection makes your code more testable. *Flashback to the part where I thought to myself "TDD is good to know, but kind of hard to do on a daily basis"* So knee me on the groin here, self! In retrospect, as I was brushing up on design patterns, I came across this article about the brain drain in enterprise dev.

Basically it says that enterprise devs are so outdated with respect to skills. Everybody loves lists, so here's me trying to be funny:

How to know you're interviewing an enterprise dev/zombie:

  • Instead of telling you even some anecdotal knowledge of the recent technologies/patterns/stuff they will just claim the ability to learn fast.
  • "Singleton" is their favorite design pattern. When you ask them what the fundamental problem is with singletons, they will not know. (Now do you GET THE PICTURE? *nudge nudge* I'm talking about the caption on the Alice picture.)

I digress. So here I am not worrying about TDD, trying to learn about Dependency Injection and *BOOM* they're brothers.

 

Now I'm watching the youtube video from that Quora list, willing to suck it up be a man. A programmer man, who's willing to admit he's just learning about TDD. Because all roads lead to TDD. Fuck.
