Sometimes on Eclipse, you would encounter errors that are just so frustrating that you almost pull your hair out. Then you check, double-check, and triple-check your code and your syntax and you see absolutely nothing wrong with it. Looks like this:

[caption id="attachment_28" align="aligncenter" width="374"] Second most common cause of hair loss in programmers.[/caption]

You know that sometimes Eclipse just screws up sometimes and all you need is a Clean command, and-slash-or a reboot of Eclipse. You do that, and the error's still there. You reboot your machine, the error's still there. Don't pull your hair out just yet.

All you have to do when you're definitely absolutely sure that there are no errors and Eclipse is just being weird is this:

Cut the line in question. Paste it on the same line.

That's it. That's all it takes. You just have to delete the line so Eclipse resets the so-called "error" that's supposedly there. When you paste the code back in, Eclipse will agree with you that there is indeed no error on that line. Try it!
