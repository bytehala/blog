This is not about changing the icons, most launchers can do that, although you can also do that using this technique.

What this is about, is changing the images INSIDE of an android app, including the ones it puts on your notification bar. Like a boss.

I installed Smart App Protector on my phone, which is running Zeus ROM 7. It starts a service that leaves fugly icon of a padlock on your notification bar. It did not look good with Zeus's snazzy icons*:

[caption id="attachment_72" align="aligncenter" width="307"] Upper left. I know.[/caption]

*Screenshot is not Zeus. The image is only to show the ugly icon. heh

So I replaced the image using APK MultiTool and my awesome artistic talents! Here's the result*:

[caption id="attachment_73" align="aligncenter" width="269"] Blue padlock! What sorcery is this?![/caption]

*Actual Zeus ROM from my actual phone.

HOW TO DO IT?

1) Get the APK.

2) Send the APK to your PC's <apkmultitooldirectory>/place-apk-here-for-modding

3) Run <apkmultitooldirectory>/Script.bat, press a key, and you'll find yourself here:

[caption id="attachment_74" align="aligncenter" width="687"] Which one was for simple tasks such as image editing again?[/caption]

4) Choose 24 - Set current project. You will be prompted to pick a .apk file that you want to edit. After that you can now do 1 - Extract apk.

5) The apk gets extracted to <apkmultitooldirectory>/projects/

6) Find the image you don't like, replace it with an image you like. Good example: blue_padlock.jpg Bad example: boobs.png

7) Go back to the multitool and select 3 - Zip apk, then 4 - Sign apk. The new and improved apk is now in <apkmultitooldirectory>/projects/place-apk-here-for-modding/signed_<apk_name>.apk

DONE!

It's not as hard as I probably make it look, you should try it for yourself! Of course, if your phone/tablet is connected to your PC, it can probably take less steps (See option 7 on the screenshot), but you get the idea.
